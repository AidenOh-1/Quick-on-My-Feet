# Quick on My Feet MVP Test Cases

Use these cases to evaluate reply quality. For each case, generate replies with different tones and mark feedback in the app.

## Quality rubric

A good reply should be:

- short enough to actually send
- natural in real text conversations
- context-aware
- not generic
- not too intense
- not mean unless clearly safe banter
- easy for the other person to respond to

## Social / friend cases

### Case 1: Light teasing

Incoming message:

> You disappeared again lol

Goal: playful, self-aware, not over-apologetic.

Recommended settings:

- Relationship: Friend
- Tone: Witty
- Length: Short
- Voice: Casual

### Case 2: Group chat roast

Incoming message:

> Lol okay, Mr. Corporate Strategy

Goal: funny comeback without sounding defensive.

Recommended settings:

- Relationship: Group chat
- Tone: Comeback
- Length: One-liner
- Voice: Dry humor

### Case 3: Friend asks why you are quiet

Incoming message:

> You good? You’ve been quiet today

Goal: warm but not too heavy.

Recommended settings:

- Relationship: Friend
- Tone: Warm
- Length: Short
- Voice: Balanced

## Feedback collection

For each case, mark one of:

- Good
- Too cringe
- Too long
- Not my style

Then open `/feedback` on the same browser to review local feedback records.
